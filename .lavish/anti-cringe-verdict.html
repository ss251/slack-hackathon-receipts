02

Why every culture-bot curdles — the framework

Not just a dunk. Three laws that generalize, so we never wander back in.

LAW 1

Byproduct, not objective

Fun & belonging are second-order effects of doing something else together. Anything whose stated purpose is "produce belonging" advertises intent — and visible intent-to-manufacture-emotion reads as manipulation.

LAW 2

Fun dies top-down; utility survives

Loved things spread sideways (a pasted Wordle grid, a blob emoji). A bot is structurally top-down — someone with admin installed it. Nobody resents the CI bot; fun payloads don't survive the same delivery.

LAW 3

Status flows to the user, not the bot

Inside jokes & emoji are valuable because they're scarce, earned, unadministered. Minting them on demand prints the currency until it's worthless.

Cool tools transfer status to their user.  Cringe tools perform personality for their owner.
Build the weapon. Skip the ceremony.

The move — kill the fun lane, build this

Summoned weapon · chosen venue · zero ceremony

🔁 Third Time — the community answer desk

In a Slack you chose (OSS project, cohort, hackathon workspace), a maintainer flicks 🔁 on yet-another-repeat-question. The agent answers from the community's own conversation history with permalinks — and after the third repeat, drafts the FAQ entry.

🚪 Door 1 — chosen venue

A community, not a company. No HR in the building = nothing to be HR-cringe about.

⚔️ Door 2 — summoned weapon

Status flows to the maintainer (their old answer cited like precedent) & the asker (ask the archive, save face). Nobody says "culture."

  • Real demand, their own words: "everyone burns out faster b/c the same questions get asked" · "revisit a question I asked last week and couldn't find it" — a decade of HN receipts.
  • RTS is genuinely load-bearing: answers only from what the asker can see + writes back to the FAQ — a stale-index docs bot can't do it. (kapa.ai answers from docs; this answers from the conversations that never became docs.)
  • The judges ARE the persona — Slack/Salesforce DevRel live this exact pain in their own community Slacks. Agent-for-Good, credibly thin field.
  • The real test it passes: the one idea from this whole run you'd still be using the week after judging.

🥈 Runner-up — Receipts (the spicy one)

Mid-argument, anyone types @receipts → the agent returns the prior decision: who, when, exact quote, live permalink, only from what you can see. Best single demo moment in the space — "the bot just ended him with a link from March" is a screenshot people actually post. It inverts the surveillance dread: the bot holds the org accountable to its own public words.

⚠️ Honest land mine: this shares territory with the killed Contradiction Catcher. The difference is structural — that was a push-nanny firing warnings on a ritual nobody performs; this is a pull-weapon you summon when you want the receipt right now. But if your "no value" was about the whole decision-memory area (not just the push mechanics), this dies too. That's the gut-check below.
