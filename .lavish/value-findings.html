02

Why every idea hit the same wall

The structural headwind: the required tech (RTS / MCP) isn't a moat — it's a commodity Slack itself is deploying across all of these exact workflows in 2026 (30 new AI features: personal digests, commitment tracking, decision logging to Linear via MCP, an onboarding agent template, expert-finding in Enterprise Search). Any third-party "agent" built on the same primitives is, by construction, a feature Slack is racing to ship natively and better — which guts both differentiation and criterion 2 ("wouldn't be possible without the required tech"). The more obvious and valuable the idea, the more certain Slack has already built it.

The 7 candidates — and the exact prior art that kills each

Deduped from 23 raw concepts. Ranked by value_ovr. None cleared the bar; the honest weakness is stated first.

Amber crisis-flag → route to a human mod · Good
5.5 · WEAK
Severe but rare + tech-first. Peer-support lives on Discord, so the Slack user base is tiny and crisis events fire rarely — fails "frequent + widely felt." And RTS isn't load-bearing: crisis detection reads the message stream, routing is a presence lookup — neither needs permission-aware search. High safety liability.
prior art: Discord "Suicide Prevention Bot" (mature, ML); 18Percent runs this on Slack already
Who Would Know find + warm-intro the teammate who solved this · Either
5.4 · WEAK
The moat is illusory. Content-aware expert matching in Slack is already owned by Starmind (acquired), Glean ($4B), and native Slack Enterprise Search ("identify SMEs from their contributions"). The RTS-as-you angle is self-contradicting — to match on convos the asker can't see you'd need cross-permission indexing (= Starmind's approach, and a leak risk). Episodic lookup, not a weekly ritual.
prior art: Starmind, Glean, native Enterprise Search, Stack Overflow for Teams
First Shift volunteer onboarding/retention concierge · Good
5.4 · WEAK
Both legs compromised. The delightful leg ("celebrate the impact you made") needs per-volunteer data that lives in a VMS/spreadsheet, not Slack → degrades to generic "thanks!". The reliable leg (answer from past threads) is exactly Slack's own official Onboarding Agent Template — a judge sees Slack's canned demo with a nonprofit skin.
prior art: Slack Onboarding Agent Template, Agentforce Channel Expert, GreetBot/CultureBot/Donut
Nice Catch auto-detect invisible work → prompt a specific thank-you · Either
5.2 · WEAK
A vitamin, but with a real moat. Under-recognition is diffuse (nobody begs for it), and the person who feels the pain isn't who installs it → no self-serve loop. BUT its one defensible claim held up: every recognition incumbent (HeyTaco, Bonusly, Matter) is genuinely still manual — auto-detection is real whitespace. Bot-prompted thanks may be worth less than spontaneous.
prior art: incumbents are manual (moat real); but Question Base + UpAgents + Claude Tag circling the auto-detect angle
Loose Ends personal open-loops copilot (the dropped ask) · Orgs
5.0 · WEAK
The strongest pain, the deadest lane. This was the #1 most emotionally-charged wedge in the whole corpus — and Slack's own Slackbot now ships both halves natively (personal digest of unanswered requests + auto-logged commitments), free, with more context than any RTS app. It's a third-party rebuild of a flagship native feature, pitched to the company that just shipped it.
prior art: native Slackbot 2026 + ClearFeed, Follow Up Bot, Chaser
Make It Real capture a decision + write it to your tool of record · Orgs
4.8 · WEAK
Load-bearing differentiator is factually false. The claimed gap ("integrations capture a ticket but drop the WHY") is filled by Rekap (near-verbatim clone), Decision Tracker, Loqbooq, Elium. Decision-frequency-gated (not a daily habit), and the space's gravity pulls straight toward the nag/governance flavor you rejected.
prior art: Rekap, Decision Tracker, Decision Desk, Loqbooq + native Jira/Linear/Notion Slack agents
Dispatch mutual-aid need → volunteer matching · Good
4.8 · WEAK
Mis-models the domain + already built free. Real dispatch runs on structured intake + a maintained roster + geocoding, not free-text chat — so "extract availability from conversation" is both demo-fragile and a worse data model → RTS is decoration. Groups are budget-less and mostly on Discord+Airtable.
prior art: open-source astoria-tech/volunteer-dispatch is the exact concept, free
04

My honest read

Why this is hard to win well

  • Slack's 2026 platform pitch is "agents work here" — they're first-partying every high-value workflow.
  • RTS/MCP are commodities, not moats — the differentiation has to come from the idea, and the obvious ideas are colonized.
  • The judges are Slack/Salesforce DevRel — they know exactly what's on their own roadmap.
  • Chasing the rubric got us a hollow idea; chasing value got us saturated ones. That's a real signal about this specific hackathon.

Where a real opening still exists

  • A lane Slack won't build: Slack ships enterprise utility, never fun / culture / delight. That lane is genuinely unexplored here and it's where you have taste.
  • Distribution, not novelty: the Good track rewards giving a community a paid-gated capability free — value = access, and Slack has no incentive to serve budget-less orgs.
  • Nice Catch is the one with a verified structural moat (every incumbent is manual) — if it's reframed from vitamin toward a ritual you'd personally love.
The bigger question worth asking out loud: you're also holding the Reddit "Games with a Hook" hackathon (Jul 15, $40K + $5K Phaser) — a creative/fun/community arena with far less first-party saturation and much more room for taste and delight. Same ~8-day window. It may simply be a better use of your build time than fighting Slack's roadmap. That's a live option, not a distraction.
