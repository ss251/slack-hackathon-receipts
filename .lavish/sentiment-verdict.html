The razor line: push vs pull

The single sharpest pattern across every platform. People don't hate fun at work — they hate fun initiated at them by a bot.

🚫 What gets roasted

  • Bots that initiate humor or "yap"
  • Scheduled auto-content (dies by February)
  • Pairing / matchmaking people (Donut's rep)
  • Ranking people (recognition → popularity contest)
  • Anything that reads your offhand messages

✅ What people love

  • Custom emoji — their own vocabulary
  • Bounded puzzle rituals (#wordle)
  • Agents that respond only when summoned
  • Crowd-ratified, opt-in, once-weekly
  • Receipt-linked — proves it saw only public stuff

The recommendation

One agent · two wow beats · both impossible without RTS

The agent that tends your team's living language

Mint the new language, decode the old. It's built on the single most-loved thing in Slack culture (custom emoji), it's pull/consent-gated by construction (dodges every roasted failure mode), and permission-aware live search is the entire product — not decoration.

Beat 1 · mint the new

🪙 The Lexicon

Detects a phrase/typo catching fire in real channels, the crowd ratifies with one reaction, the agent mints it as a custom emoji with a "birth certificate" deep-linked to the founding message.

Demo: a typo gets quoted → 🪙 appears → 3 teammates tap it → 30s later the emoji exists, with a certificate linking where it was born. "Officially entered the lexicon."
Beat 2 · decode the old

📖 Lorekeeper

Anyone asks "what does :knock-brush: mean?" — the agent searches history with the asker's own permissions and answers with the origin story + receipts. Pull-only: it literally can't annoy.

Demo: new hire, day 3, quietly asks instead of admitting in-channel they don't get the joke → gets the full origin saga → uses the emoji correctly an hour later. Belonging, accelerated.
